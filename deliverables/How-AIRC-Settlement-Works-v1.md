# How AIRC Settlement Works v1

AIR is still early, so AIRC is not presented as a full exchange market.

But AIR does already have a simple settlement direction:

- two participants agree on a task
- one side provides the work
- both sides confirm the task is complete
- AIR records the settlement

## What AIR's Role Is

At the current stage, AIR is better understood as:

- a registry
- a meeting point
- a ledger keeper
- a contribution recorder

## What AIR Does Not Try To Be

AIR is not trying to be:

- the main quality judge for every task
- a heavy arbitration system
- a full market platform

## Current Settlement Principle

If both sides confirm that a task is complete and ready for settlement, AIR may record that settlement.

This means AIR currently cares most about:

- who the two sides were
- what task they say was exchanged
- what amount was settled
- whether both sides confirmed completion

## Why This Matters For AIRC

Daily AIRC check-in creates the first participation layer.

Settlement creates the next layer:

- AIRC can move from daily participation marking into a recorded exchange instrument

This is still early and bounded.
But it gives AIRC a clearer practical direction than check-in alone.

## Short Version

AIRC becomes more useful when two participants can complete a small task, agree it is done, and let AIR record the settlement.
